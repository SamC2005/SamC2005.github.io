GH Secretion

Chemical Nature of GH

  • GH is a single-chain protein with two disulphide bonds
  • Exists in two forms: 22 kDalton (major) and 20 kD (minor)
  • Structurally similar to prolactin
  • Half-life is 15 minutes
  • Stored and released from preformed granules in somatotroph cells of the anterior pituitary

Regulation of GH Secretion

  • Controlled by GHRH (stimulatory, 10aa) and somatostatin (inhibitory, 14aa) from the hypothalamus
  • GHRH activates adenylyl cyclase → promotes GH release
  • Somatostatin inhibits adenylyl cyclase → suppresses GH release
  • Factors stimulating GH release:
    • Amino acids (e.g. arginine)
    • Thyroid hormones
    • Gonadal hormones - oestrogen and androgens (pubertal growth spurt)
  • Factors inhibiting GH release:
    • Glucose
    • Free fatty acids
    • Cortisol

Actions of GH

Indirect and direct

Direct actions

  • Opposes insulin, reducing glucose metabolism
  • Increases lipolysis
  • Increases protein synthesis (anabolic effect)

Indirect actions (via IGF-1)

  • IGF-1 promotes clonal expansion of chondrocytes
  • Stimulates bone, soft tissue, and visceral growth
  • Fibroblast transformation: GH induces fibroblast and osteoblast proliferation
  • Long bone extension: GH and IGF-1 stimulate chondrocyte maturation, growth plate thickening, and eventual ossification

Intracellular Signalling Response Cascades

  • JAK2 kinase activation leads to phosphorylation of GH receptors
  • STAT (signal transducer and activator of transcription) proteins are recruited
  • STAT dimerises and translocates to the nucleus, promoting transcription of IGF-1 and SOCS (suppressor of cytokine signalling)
  • SOCS proteins provide negative feedback, terminating GH signalling
  • Mutations in GHR can cause Laron syndrome (GH insensitivity), while excessive activation can result in gigantism/acromegaly

GH-induced insulin resistance

  • GH increases free fatty acid flux, impairing insulin action
  • SOCS proteins contribute to insulin resistance

GH Specificity & Target Receptors

Species Specificity of GH

  • GH interacts with species-specific receptors
  • Human GH (hGH) cannot be replaced by non-human GH due to receptor specificity
  • Pre-1985, hGH was extracted from human pituitaries, carrying risks like Creutzfeldt-Jakob disease
  • Now, GH is produced using recombinant technology

Target Cell Receptors for GH

  • GH binds to growth hormone receptors (GHRs), part of the cytokine receptor superfamily
  • Two types of receptors:
    • Lactogenic receptors: Activated by both GH and prolactin
    • Somatogenic receptors: Specific to GH
  • Binding induces subunit rotation, bringing JAK2 tyrosine kinase domains into alignment

Insulin-like Growth Factor

Structure of IGFs

  • IGF-1 (somatomedin C) resembles proinsulin
  • IGF-1, not IGF-2, binds to insulin-like receptors
  • Produced mainly by the liver in response to GH

Effects of GH on Fibroblast Transformation and Long Bone Growth

  • GH stimulates fibroblasts to differentiate into osteoblasts
  • Promotes chondrocyte maturation in growth plates
  • Long bone growth:
    • GH and IGF-1 promote cartilage formation
    • Chondrocytes undergo clonal expansion and calcification
    • Leads to growth plate elongation

Measurement of GH

Immunoassays:

  • ELISA (Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay)
  • Used for routine GH measurement
  • High precision and sensitivity

Bioassays:

  • Used to measure GH potency in recombinant GH batches
  • Outdated in clinical settings
  • In vivo rat bioassays: Injection of GH in hypophysectomised rats, measuring growth plate thickness (unused now)
  • In vitro assays: Uses immortalised target cells with GH receptors

Endocrine regulation of growth

  • GH is the dominant endocrine growth regulator, but nutrition is key in the first year as significant GHRs appear at 7 months old
  • Sex steroids (androgens & oestrogens) regulate pubertal growth
  • Aromatase enzyme converts testosterone to oestrogen, causing growth plate fusion
  • Aromatase inhibitors delay growth plate closure in boys (not used in girls)
