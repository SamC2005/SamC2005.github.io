Hypothalamo-Pituitary-Adrenal (HPA) axis

Circadian cortisol rhythm:

Cortisol is lowest in evening, highest in morning.

If someone is stressed, they see higher levels at night too, with a small peak in morning

POMC Processing

  • Expression of pre-POMC driven by CRH
  • Cleave single peptide to generate POMC
  • Cleave POMC (with PHC/endopeptidase):
    • Pro-ACTH + β-LPH
  • Further PHC/endopeptidase action:
    • ACTH
    • MSH (ɑ-, β-, γ-)
    • β-endorphins

Adrenal Organisation

Each layer produces slightly different hormones

Mnemonic for order of layers: Sits on kidney, kidney has GFR (glomerular filtration rate)

Blood supply

Capsule, then cortical a., then medullary a., then cortical & medullary capillaries, medullary v., central vein

The hormones

Synthesis

RED - Zona glomerulosa - aldosterone

BLUE - Zona fasciculata - cortisol

BLACK - Zona reticularis - DHEA

Aldosterone - key to regulating Na/K balance

Cortisol - long term stress, circadian rhythm

Metabolic Actions of cortisol

Increase plasma glucose

  • Inhibit IMGD via GLUT4
  • Spare glucose metabolism
  • Increase hepatic gluconeogenesis
  • Increase variability of gluconeogenic substrates
    • Glycerol
    • Free amino-acids

Sparing glucose metabolism

  • Some tissues reliant on glycolysis (brain, erythrocytes, renal medulla)
  • Most tissues capable of using non-carbohydrates

Hepatic gluconeogenesis

Must overcome irreversible glycolysis steps:

  • Glucose —> glucose-6-P
    • (hexokinase)
  • Fructose-1-P —> Fructose-1,6-P
    • (phosphofructokinase)
  • Phosphenol pyruvate (PEP) —> pyruvate

Cortisol activates GR to increase expression of gluconeogenic enzymes:

  • Glucose-6-P —> glucose
    • (glucose-6-phosphatase)
  • Fructose-1,6-P —> Fructose-1-P
    • (fructose-1,6-bisphosphatase)
  • Oxaloacetate (OAA) —> PEP
    • (PEPCK = PEP corboxykinase)

Non-Metabolic Actions of Cortisol

  • Negative feedback (CRH + ACTH)
  • Positive inotropic action on heart, abnormal ECG
  • Increase renal blood flow/GFR/water balance
  • Inhibits fibroblast proliferation/collagen forming
    • increased bruising/impaired wound healing
  • Actions on mood and behaviour
  • Increase synthesis of surfactant
  • Anti-inflammatory/immunosuppressant
  • Increase serum calcium (bone catabolism)

Adrenal dysfunction

  • Cushing’s disease
  • Cushing’s syndrome
  • Addison’s disease
  • Adrenal insufficiency
  • Congenital adrenal hyperplasia (CAH)
  • Primary hyperaldosterism (Conn’s syndrome)

Adrenal dysfunction - altered hormone actions

  • Pseudohypoaldosteronism (PHA)
  • Glucocorticoid resistance
  • Apparent mineralocorticoid excess (AME)

Pathogenesis

  • Increased or decreased endocrine drive
  • Aberrant expression and/or activities of steroidogenic enzymes
  • Destruction of 1 or more adrenal zones
    • e.g. autoimmunity
  • Defective steroid receptors
    • e.g. specificity, binding, TAF activity
  • Iatrogenic syndromes

Symptoms

Increased corticosteroid action

Hyperglycaemia

  • Increased gluconeogenesis
  • Increased glycogenolysis
  • Increased protein catabolism

Insulin resistance

Redistribution of body fat

  • Increased lipolysis in extremities (HSL)
  • Increased central lipogenesis
  • Moon face + buffalo hump + abdominal adiposity

Increased bruising (weakened vessel walls)

Immune suppression

  • Anti-inflammatory action (PLA2)
  • Lymphatic/thymus involution
  • Antagonise cytokine action

Gastric ulceration

Osteoporosis

Hyperandrogenism

  • Acne, male-pattern baldness + hirsutism
  • Female pseudohermaphroditism
  • ?Precocious puberty

In males, DHEA swamped by testosterone so has no effect

In women, responsible for adrenarche, and an oestrogen substrate for post-menopausal women

Electrolyte and fluid imbalance

  • Hypokalaemia (?neuropathy)
  • Anti-natriuresis

Hypervolemic hypertension

  • (headaches/visual disturbances/?stroke)

Decreased corticosteroid action

Hypoglycaemia

  • tiredness and weakness
  • vomiting

Hyper-pigmentation

  • particularly skin creases + mouth

Hypoandrogenism

  • Symptoms only evident in females
  • Failed adrenarche
  • Sparse axillary + pubic hair growth

Electrolyte and fluid imbalance

  • Natriuresis/hyponatremia (?neuropathy)
  • Anti-kaliuresis

Polyuria (leading to polydipsia)

Hypovolemic hypotension
