Thyroid structure

  • Gland wraps around trachea
  • Receives blood supply from thyrocervical trunk and external carotid artery
  • Middle connected by isthmus, with optional pyramidal lobe
  • 4 parathyroid glands that sense Ca2+ and secrete PTH

Morphology

Embryological abnormalities

  • Failure of gland to develop e.g. PAX8 mutation —> congenital hypothyroidism
  • Migration of thyroid —> lingual, retrosternal thyroid
  • Failure of thyroglossal dict to atrophy —> thyroglossal cyst

Thyroid hormones

Synthesis

  • Two thyroid follicles - T4, T3
  • C-cells - calcitonin

Calcitonin

  • peptide hormone
  • antagonises PTH: ↓Ca
  • Probably not significant in human Ca metabolism

TSH-R Stimulation

  • ↑ cAMP via GSɑ/adenylate cyclase
  • ↑ DNA synthesis
  • ↑ Protein synthesis
    • including thyroglobulin
  • ↑ Iodide influx into the cell via NIS (sodium-iodide symporter)
  • ↑ Thyroid hormone synthesis
    • ↑ iodination of thyroglobulin
    • ↑ microvilli number and length at luminal cell surface
    • ↑ in intracellular volume and endocytosis of colloid droplets
  • ↑ Thyroid hormone release

Thyroid hormone raw materials

  • Tyrosines
    • Glycoprotein scaffold called thyroglobulin (1 thyroglbulin = 134 tyrosines)
    • Synthesised by thyroid epithelial cells and secreted into lumen
    • Colloid is a pool of thyroglobulin
  • Iodide
    • Avidly taken up by thyroid epithelial cells
    • Via sodium-iodide symporter
    • Once inside the cell, iodide is transported into the lumen of the follicle along with thyroglobulin via pendrin

Thyroid peroxidase

  • Synthesises thyroid hormones
  • Two sequential reactions:

Hormones in circulation

  • Secretion stimulated by TSH
  • Normal ranges:
    • TSH = 0.3 - 4.5 mU/L
    • Tota T4 = 60 - 150 nmol/L
    • Total T3 = 1.2 - 2.9 nmol/L

99.9% of T3/T4 transported bound to 3 binding proteins:

  • Thyroxine binding protein (TBG) 75%
    • Binds both T3 and T4
  • Transthyreitin 20%
  • Albumin 5%
  • Only 0.1% is free (can access thyroid hormone receptors)

Physiological actions

  1. Increase basal metabolism
  1. Promote growth and development
  1. Cardiovascular
    • Positively chronotropic, inotropic
  1. CNS
    • Regulates alertness
    • Development of foetal brain
  1. Modulate actions of other hormones

Deiodination

T4 is a pro hormone for T3

  • T4 deiodinated to T3
  • T3 acts on nuclear receptors

Thyroid receptors

  • Nuclear receptors
    • members of steroid-thyroid hormone receptor superfamily
    • bound to DNA in their resting state
  • Bind to TR response elements (TREs)

Control of transcription

A: When no T3, TR recruits and binds corepressors to signal to histones to close up so no transcription (gene off)

C: If no TR bound to TRE (or T3), there can be some transcription going through gene (base metabolism) —> (gene “half on”)

Types of TR

  • Two genes
    • TRɑ
    • TRβ
  • Each can be selectively spliced
    • TRɑ1,2
    • TRβ1,2
  • Tissue specific

T3 effect on mitochondria

  • Mitochondria has its own transcription machinery
  • Thyroid hormone receptor binds to mitochondria as thyroid hormone controls basal metabolism
